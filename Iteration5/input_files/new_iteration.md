- Justification: The current results provide a clear "negative" finding that is scientifically valuable: the "Lévy flight" hypothesis, as traditionally modeled via CTRW and $Q$-parameter classification, is not supported by the data at the resolved scales. This is not a failure of the project, but a refinement of the physical understanding of 2D turbulence. The "Publishability Delta" is high because the paper can shift from a "confirmatory" study of Lévy flights to a "critical re-evaluation" of Lagrangian transport in 2D turbulence. By implementing the suggested methodological shifts—specifically replacing the noisy $Q$-parameter with FTLE-based ridge identification and moving from hard-threshold wavelet filtering to smooth spectral filtering—the project addresses the fundamental structural weaknesses identified. The ROI is high because the infrastructure (DNS, tracer data, and analysis pipeline) is already robust; the effort is now focused on more physically grounded diagnostic techniques (conditional probability analysis near FTLE ridges) rather than chasing non-physical scaling exponents. This iteration will likely move the manuscript from a "Borderline" to a "Strong Accept" by providing a rigorous, nuanced explanation of why previous "Lévy" claims in 2D turbulence may have been artifacts of resolution or methodology.
