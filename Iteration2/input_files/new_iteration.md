- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current results provide a strong methodological proof-of-concept but fail to capture the intended physical phenomenon (the evolution of Lévy statistics during spectral condensation) due to the "pinned" inverse cascade at $k \approx 3$. The "So What?" factor is currently limited by this lack of dynamic range. However, the infrastructure for wavelet-based eddy-lifetime scaling is now functional and robust. By implementing the suggested changes—specifically moving the forcing to $k \in [10, 12]$ to allow for a genuine inverse cascade development within the $512^2$ grid, and replacing the unreliable Hill estimator with CCDF power-law analysis—the project will move from a "quasi-stationary" observation to a dynamic, high-impact study of how transport statistics evolve with the inverse cascade. The ROI of effort is high because the simulation and analysis pipelines are already written; the required changes are parameter adjustments and diagnostic refinements rather than a complete project overhaul.
