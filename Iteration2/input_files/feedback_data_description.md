The failure to observe the full development of the inverse cascade (the pinning of $k_{peak} \approx 3$) is directly attributable to the simulation design constraints. The research plan specified a forcing band of $k \in [3, 5]$ and a spinup time of $T_{spinup} = 10.0$. In 2D turbulence, the inverse cascade develops as energy moves from the forcing scale toward the fundamental wavenumber ($k=1$). By forcing at $k \in [3, 5]$ and limiting the spinup to $T=10$, the simulation did not provide sufficient time or spectral space for the energy to condense at the domain scale ($k_{peak} \to 1$). This constraint prevented the analysis of $\alpha(t)$ evolution during spectral condensation, as the Eulerian flow structure remained effectively static throughout the production run.
