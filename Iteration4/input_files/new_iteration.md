- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current results have successfully established a critical scientific finding: $\alpha(t)$ is non-stationary and linked to the spectral condensation of the inverse cascade. However, the wavelet-based filtering approach has reached a point of diminishing returns, as it fails to capture the underlying Lagrangian dynamics (likely due to the destruction of hyperbolic manifolds). The proposed changes—shifting from wavelet filtering to Lagrangian Coherent Structure (LCS) analysis (FTLE/Okubo-Weiss) and analyzing residence/flight time distributions—directly address the "Why" behind the observed non-stationarity. This shift moves the paper from a descriptive study of $\alpha(t)$ to a mechanistic explanation of the physical drivers (vortex death/strain stripping), which significantly increases the "Publishability Delta" and scientific impact. The ROI is high because the existing simulation data is already of high quality; the effort is focused on re-analyzing the existing velocity fields using more physically appropriate geometric tools rather than performing a new, expensive simulation.
