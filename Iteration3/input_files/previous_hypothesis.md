The stationarity of Lévy flight statistics ($\alpha$) is not an intrinsic property of the turbulence but a consequence of the spectral condensation state, specifically the ratio of the forcing scale $k_f$ to the peak energy scale $k_{peak}(t)$. I hypothesize that by introducing a scale-dependent "eddy-memory" filter—where tracers are advected by a velocity field reconstructed only from wavelet coefficients corresponding to the current $k_{peak}(t)$—we can isolate the contribution of the inverse cascade's growth to the observed drift in $\alpha(t)$. Furthermore, I propose that the unphysical results from the Hill estimator in previous iterations were due to the inclusion of small-scale "noise" displacements; by applying a wavelet-based denoising threshold (retaining only coefficients above the local RMS velocity at scale $j$), the Hill estimator will converge with the McCulloch method, revealing that the "anomalous" nature of the transport is dominated by the coherent structures (vortices) at the $k_{peak}$ scale rather than the background turbulent field.
