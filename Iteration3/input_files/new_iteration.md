- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current results are scientifically compromised by a "scale collapse" where the inverse cascade reaches the box scale almost immediately, rendering the non-stationarity analysis trivial and the $\alpha \approx 1.0$ result an artifact of large-scale vortex dominance rather than turbulent transport. However, the framework for wavelet-based eddy-lifetime scaling and the non-stationarity analysis methodology are sound. The failure is purely one of simulation design (insufficient scale separation). By strictly adhering to the $1024^2$ resolution requirement and shifting the forcing to $k \in [30, 40]$, we can create the necessary inertial range to observe the evolution of $\alpha(t)$ during the transient cascade phase. This iteration is essential because the current manuscript is currently unpublishable due to the lack of dynamic range, but the proposed structural changes (restoring scale separation and focusing on the transient phase) directly address the "Structural Robustness" and "Reviewer Confidence" criteria, moving the project from a failed experiment to a high-impact study of non-stationary transport.
